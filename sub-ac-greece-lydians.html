The Lydians

Lydia arose as a kingdom from the remains of the Hittite empire, which collapsed in the 12th century BCE. The Lydians lived in the western part of what is now Turkey. Ancient Greek historians such as Herodotus tell us that the Lydians were the first Mediterranean people to develop coins as money. They minted gold and silver coins that could be exchanged for goods.

The Lydians were also the first Mediterranean people to establish retail shops in permanent locations. But surely their shops contained different goods than our shops of today… what do you think they contained?

The most famous Lydian king was called Croesus. Croesus’s kingdom clashed with the Persians in the east. Before the war with the Persians, Croesus asked for the advice of the oracle at Delphi. The Delphic oracle was known for giving advice that could have many different meanings. The oracle advised Croesus that if he attacked the Persians, he would destroy a great empire… but the oracle did not tell Croesus which empire he would destroy! He went to war with the Persians, and it was his own empire that was destroyed!
