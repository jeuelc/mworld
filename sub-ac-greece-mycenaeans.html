The Mycenaeans were the first Greek speaking population to live in Greece. The poetry of Homer suggests that the Mycenaens were a society organized around a king and his court.

The Mycenaens had extensive trade relationships with the Minoans. Archaeologists have found many Minoan records written in Linear B, the language of the Mycenaeans. It took historians more than 50 years to decode the writing code of Linear B. Become an archaeologist one day, and maybe you too can solve 3000 year old codes!

Mycenaean artists were skilful in crafting jewellery and art from gold and other precious metals. They also had amazing engineering ability. The entrance to the city at Mycenae was protected via the famous lion gate. The stone above the gate weighed more than a rhinoceros, or a large car!

We still don't know how the Mycenaens managed to lift the stone into place above the gate. Can you think of a way of lifting such a large stone high up into the air?
