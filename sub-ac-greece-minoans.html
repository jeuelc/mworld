The Minoans

The Minoan Civilisation flourished on the island of Crete during the third and second millennium BCE. The Minoans are named after King Minos, allegedly a son of Zeus.

The Minoans were discovered in modern times by Sir Arthur Evans, a British archaeologist. Evans excavated the site of Knossos in Crete, discovering a palace belonging to a distinct, pre-Greek civilisation... the Minoans! Many other Minoan palaces have been excavated in other parts of Crete since then.

The Minoans established a lively culture based on trade and had a tradition in sport, dancing, jewellery-making and weaving. Bulls were sacred to them as was the famous snake goddess.
