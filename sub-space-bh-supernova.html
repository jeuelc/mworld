Supernova

When a supergiant star runs out of fuel, it starts to collapse. The collapse squeezes and heats the remains of the star, causing a series of massive explosions, called a supernova!

A supernova explosion is so violent that the star is torn apart, blowing hydrogen, helium and other elements out into space. These elements forms the materials that make up the rest of the Universe, including the Earth and our very own bodies!

In this sense, we are all made from star dust. Did you know that gold also comes from the dust of a supernova?!
