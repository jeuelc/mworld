Neutron Star

click here click here

When a supernova has exploded, the star’s core collapses. This forms a neutron star, or if the core keeps collapsing, it forms a black hole.

A neutron star is a small but very dense star, made up of very small particles called neutrons.

Being dense means that a little bit of neutron star weighs an awful lot. A piece of neutron star the size of a grain of sand would weigh as much as a large airplane!

Despite being very heavy, neutron stars can spin around very fast. Some neutron stars can spin around hundreds of times each second!

A thousand years ago there was a large supernova explosion that was so bright it could be seen on earth without any telescopes! The remains of this supernova are called Crab Nebula and they contain a small, very dense neutron star.
